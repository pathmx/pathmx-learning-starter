# Using the PathMX Player

In about eight minutes, you will know how to move through a lesson, reveal
help, leave feedback, and return to the path map.

---

<!--
type: orientation
id: two-views
-->

## Browse and Play

**Browse view** shows the whole Source. Use it to scan the lesson, follow links,
or return to an earlier idea.

**Play mode** focuses on one learning Beat at a time. Use the forward and back
controls when you want a guided sequence with less visual noise.

Your agent should normally link you directly to the Source, Block, or Beat that
matters rather than asking you to find it.

## Try a staged reveal

Think of one topic you would genuinely enjoy understanding better. Then reveal
the next stage for a small planning hint and example.

<learning-reveal label="A three-stage learning prompt">
  <slot name="prompt">
    <strong>Prompt</strong>
    <p>What would you like to be able to do—not merely know—after learning it?</p>
  </slot>
  <slot name="hint">
    <strong>Hint</strong>
    <p>Use an observable verb: explain, diagnose, compare, design, repair, perform, or decide.</p>
  </slot>
  <slot name="answer">
    <strong>Example</strong>
    <p>“I can compare two home-loan offers and explain the tradeoffs in plain language.”</p>
  </slot>
</learning-reveal>

In Play mode, component stages become part of the forward sequence. Outside
Play, the button lets you reveal them directly.

## Leave feedback without stopping

Annotations let you attach a thought to the exact passage that prompted it.
Use them for confusion, disagreement, a useful connection, or a request for
more depth.

Your agent can review those notes before preparing the next module. You do not
need to wait for a reply before continuing through already prepared sessions.

If annotation controls are not visible, tell your agent what you wanted to
mark and include the relevant words or heading.

## You are ready

You now know the basic loop:

1. Open the exact link your agent shares.
2. Use Play for a focused sequence or Browse for the whole Source.
3. Use built-in reveals when you need help.
4. Annotate thoughts that can wait for the next agent review.
5. Return to [your learning-space home](../index.path.md) to see the path map.

Next, tell your agent what you want to learn and what a worthwhile result would
look like in your life.
